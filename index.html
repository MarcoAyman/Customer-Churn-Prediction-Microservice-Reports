▶ How we decided
Encoding decisions
5 feature groups
Column fate
Pipeline flow
Step 1 — Cramér's V analysis on all 7 categorical features
Cramér's V measures how strongly a categorical column predicts churn (0=none, 1=perfect). We set a threshold of V ≥ 0.15 to decide between target encoding and one-hot encoding. Features above threshold have meaningfully different churn rates across their categories — replacing them with those rates gives the model a direct risk signal.
Step 2 — Cohen's d + Pearson r on numeric features
Cohen's d measures the separation between churner and retained distributions. Pearson r identifies correlated raw columns. When two features share high correlation (e.g. order_count ↔ coupon_used, r=0.62), we create a ratio instead of keeping both — discount_dependency_ratio = coupon_used / order_count — capturing the independent signal and reducing multicollinearity.
Step 3 — Cohort analysis for binary flags
Cohort analysis showed new customers (tenure ≤ 6mo) churn at 32.4% — 1.93× the baseline. Even though this didn't clear the 2.0× hotspot threshold, the business signal is clear enough to create an explicit is_new_customer binary flag. Loyal customers (24+mo) show 0.0% churn.
Step 4 — Training-serving skew prevention
All parameters that require learning from data (churn rates, log-median, IQR cap) are fitted on the training fold only (3,940 rows). Val and test receive the same frozen parameters. Recomputing on val/test would be data leakage — making evaluation metrics dishonest.
▶ What needs fit() vs what doesn't
fit() learns statistics from training data. transform() applies them to any fold.
complain_riskfit() → churn rates {0:0.112, 1:0.313}
order_cat_riskfit() → churn rates per category
marital_riskfit() → churn rates per status
warehouse_riskfit() → log-median = 2.7081
embeddednessfit() → IQR cap = 12.0
ratio featuresno fit() needed — pure math
binary flagsno fit() needed — fixed thresholds
binned featuresno fit() needed — fixed bin edges

▶ Group 1 — Financial Stress (3 features)
FeatureFormulaEDA justification
discount_dependency_ratiocoupon_used / (order_count + ε)r=0.62 between order_count and coupon_used. Ratio captures discount reliance independent of order volume. Creates signal where the individual features overlap.

▶ Group 2 — Service Dependency (3 features)
FeatureFormulaEDA justification
platform_embeddedness_score(addr_capped × 0.4) + (devices × 0.6)number_of_device d=+0.29, address d=+0.12. Both show retained customers are more embedded. Combined into one score — devices weighted 0.6 (stronger signal). Address capped at IQR upper (12.0, fitted on train).

▶ Group 3 — Engagement Decay (3 features)
FeatureFormulaEDA justification
recency_risk_scoreday_since_last_order / (tenure + 1)day_since_last_order d=−0.42 — churners go longer without ordering. Dividing by tenure normalises: a 30-day gap means more for a 2-month customer than a 2-year customer.
order_frequency_normalizedorder_count / (tenure + 1)order_count alone d=−0.064 (negligible) because new and old customers have inherently different raw counts. Normalising isolates the frequency signal.

▶ Group 4 — Contract Risk (5 features)
FeatureFormulaEDA justification
tenure_segmentcut(tenure, [0,6,24,∞]) → 0/1/2Cohort: new=32.4%, growing=8.2%, loyal=0.0% churn. Extreme non-linearity — raw tenure would make the model learn this curve slowly. Explicit bins make it immediate.
is_new_customer1 if tenure ≤ 6Cohort lift=1.93× (32.4% vs 16.84% baseline). Separate from tenure_segment to let the model use both the continuous and discrete signals simultaneously.
silent_dissatisfaction_flag1 if score ≤ 3 AND complain == 0The "silent churner" pattern. A dissatisfied customer who never complained is hard to detect from either feature alone. Combined flag surfaces this latent risk.
complaint_satisfaction_interactioncomplain × (6 − satisfaction_score)Complained AND low satisfaction = highest risk. Produces 0 if no complaint regardless of satisfaction. Range 0–5: captures intensity, not just presence.
warehouse_distance_risk1 if log(warehouse) > 2.7081warehouse_to_home d=+0.19. EDA recommended log1p transform (right-skewed). Fitted log-median = 2.7081 from train only. Customers above median distance are flagged.
